Our Story

Nine Years Ago

Brandon and I met in high school eleven years ago through my Maid of Honour, Sara. I was practicing the clarinet before music class. I was pretty uninterested in meeting these two guys that Sara thought were pretty cool, because obviously my clarinet skills were more important.

Over the next year or so, the four of us became very good friends, but it wasn't until after graduation that Brandon and I started dating. We both moved to Kingston for college and bounced around to a few different apartments. We lived together in Kingston for a few years, adopted our kitty Basie, and drank lots of beer....oops I mean water. Then we did the whole long-distance thing, and let me tell you that "absence makes the heart grow fonder" is so not true. I eventually followed Brandon to Toronto, even though I am definitely not a city girl. We went to lots of concerts and explored the city together.

When I was offered a position in my field closer to home, I moved to Frankford with some friends, and we did the long distance thing again. Brandon joined me in Frankford a few months later and started working from home (the lucky duck). We are currently living happily in Frankford, until we buy a house where we can have some chickens, grow some vegetables, and do things that people with houses do.

Nine Years Later

I proposed to Alyssa on January 11, 2016 after she got home from work with a story of us drawn on sticky notes in a heart on the wall. I proposed to her on January 11 after she got home from work, because the first four times I was going to propose before that bombed.

The first plan was to propose at Frankford's Christmas lights, but not knowing that she invited her mom and brother Luke, so that didn't pan out. After that I thought about our first Christmas, which featured sticky notes with Christmas doodles, and drew a story of us on sticky notes and started putting it on the wall the night before Christmas Eve. Mid setup, at 4AM, she woke up and from around the corner told me to go to bed without seeing what I was up to. I couldn't leave them up, since I'd either sleep in and miss my own proposal, or she'd wake up grumpy from being woken at 4AM, so I quietly peeled them off and hid them. Then I decided I'd do it after Christmas and hide the ring in the Christmas tree, but in some freak coincidence for the first time ever Alyssa took down the Christmas tree immediately. Next came New Years Eve, but a big roll out at work and lack of sleep killed that plan when I turned into a zombie.

With all the low hanging fruit taken, I took a bit trying to think of how I could surprise Alyssa, but realized that being a hermit any suggestions would seem suspect. So in the end, I decided the best surprise would be January 11, after she got home from work. She didn't see it coming :P
